# Coloring Page Content Guidelines

Goal: kid-friendly, familiar, and low-risk content for Amazon Appstore review.

## Safe Sources

- Original artwork made for Free Draw.
- Public-domain or CC0 artwork with a saved source record.
- Classic story/fairy-tale themes drawn in a new style.
- Licensed artwork only if the license is saved with the submitted build materials.

## Avoid

- Modern movie, TV, game, toy, comic, streamer, and mascot characters.
- Character names, costumes, silhouettes, symbols, vehicles, houses, logos, catchphrases, or compositions that make a protected character recognizable.
- "Inspired by" pages that are close enough for a child to identify the source franchise.
- Modern versions of public-domain characters. Use only the public-domain source-era material, and draw it in Free Draw's own style.

## Current App Approach

- The built-in Animals, Vehicles, Nature, Places, Patterns, and Storybook pages are original procedural line art.
- Classic Sheets are bundled from Openclipart public-domain/CC0 SVGs.
- Source metadata for Classic Sheets lives in `assets/public-domain/sources.json`.

## References

- U.S. Copyright Office: public-domain works may be used freely when they are no longer under copyright protection: https://www.copyright.gov/help/faq/faq-definitions.html
- Duke Law Public Domain Day 2026: works from 1930 are open in the U.S., but later versions and trademarks can still matter: https://web.law.duke.edu/cspd/publicdomainday/2026/
- USPTO trademark basics: names and logos used to advertise a business may be trademarks: https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/basics
